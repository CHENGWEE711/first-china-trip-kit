# Publishing checklist

## Before publishing

- Confirm the post matches the platform and row in `utm-links.csv`.
- Open the final URL and confirm the correct guide loads.
- Confirm `utm_source`, `utm_medium`, `utm_campaign`, and `utm_content` are lowercase.
- Confirm the post gives useful advice before asking for a click.
- Remove absolute promises about visa-free entry, payment setup, or card acceptance.
- Confirm visuals contain no third-party app logos or government-style approval marks.
- Check community self-promotion and affiliate-link rules.
- Use the site guide URL, not a direct affiliate link, in editorial posts.

## When publishing

- Use the exact final URL assigned to that content item.
- Add the live post URL to `utm-links.csv`.
- Change `publish_status` from `ready` to `published`.
- Do not shorten links unless the platform requires it and preserves query parameters.
- Do not add another tracking system that replaces the UTM query.

## After publishing

- Test the live post link on mobile.
- Check GA4 Realtime for the expected source, medium, campaign, and content value.
- Record 24-hour sessions and conversion actions.
- Reply to real questions; do not paste the same promotional answer repeatedly.
- Record moderation, removal, or platform-review outcomes in `notes`.
- At Day 7, compare conversion quality rather than raw impressions alone.

## Success thresholds for the first test

- 100-300 qualified sessions across seven days.
- At least one measurable action from each channel worth continuing.
- A clear top landing page and top source/medium pair.
- Enough evidence to stop low-quality posting formats instead of scaling them blindly.
